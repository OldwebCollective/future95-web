# How to Recycle Old Computers Properly

Learn how to recycle that old PC you have gathering dust in your office or basement. Proper e-waste disposal is crucial for the environment and your security!

## Why Recycle Your Old Computer?

Your old computer contains valuable materials like copper, gold, and rare earth metals. It also contains hazardous materials like lead and mercury that can contaminate soil and water if disposed improperly.

**Environmental Impact:**
- 50+ million tons of e-waste generated yearly worldwide
- Mining for new materials requires significant energy
- Recycling recovers up to 95% of the materials

**Security Concerns:**
- Personal data can be recovered from recycled drives
- Identity theft risks if not properly wiped
- Legal compliance (GDPR, HIPAA)

## Step 1: Back Up Your Data

Before doing anything, make sure all your important files are safely backed up.

1. Connect an external drive or cloud storage
2. Copy all important documents, photos, and files
3. Verify the backup is complete
4. Test that files are readable

## Step 2: Securely Wipe Your Drive

Never just delete files - they can be recovered! Use proper data destruction methods.

**Options:**
- **DBAN** (Darik's Boot and Nuke) - Free, thorough, military-grade
- **Eraser** - Windows-based secure deletion
- **CCleaner Pro** - User-friendly option
- **Mac: Erase Free Space** - Built into macOS
- **Physical destruction** - For highly sensitive data

**DBAN Instructions:**
1. Download DBAN from your website
2. Create bootable USB drive
3. Boot from USB (F12 or Del during startup)
4. Select "Autonuke" for quick wipe
5. Wait 1-2 hours (depending on drive size)

## Step 3: Remove Valuable Components

Before scrapping, consider harvesting parts that still have value:

- **RAM modules** - Still useful
- **SSDs/HDDs** - Can be repurposed or sold
- **Graphics cards** - Good resale value
- **Power supply** - Often reusable
- **CPU** - Valuable for collectors

## Step 4: Find a Certified E-Waste Recycler

**Look for these certifications:**
- **e-Stewards** - International standard
- **R2** (Responsible Recycling) - US-based
- **ISO 14001** - Environmental management

**Local Options:**
- Check Earth911.com for nearby recyclers
- Best Buy accepts electronics for recycling
- Local municipality e-waste collection events
- Manufacturer take-back programs (HP, Dell, etc.)

## Step 5: Transport Safely

- Pack components to prevent damage
- Keep drives and sensitive parts together
- Store in cool, dry place before dropoff
- Get a receipt for documentation

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Don't:** Leave drives in the computer  
✅ **Do:** Remove and verify they're wiped

❌ **Don't:** Throw away without proper wiping  
✅ **Do:** Use certified data destruction tools

❌ **Don't:** Mix e-waste with regular trash  
✅ **Do:** Use certified recyclers

❌ **Don't:** Forget backup before wiping  
✅ **Do:** Triple-check your backup first

## Creative Reuse Ideas

Before recycling, consider:
- **Donate to schools** - Still useful for learning
- **Set up a media center** - Use as HTPC (Home Theater PC)
- **Linux machine** - Older hardware runs Linux great
- **Retro gaming** - Perfect for emulation
- **Server** - Home NAS or personal cloud

## E-Waste Statistics

Did you know?
- One computer contains more gold than 17 tons of gold ore
- Recycling one computer saves enough energy to power 2 homes for a year
- Only 12.5% of electronics are properly recycled globally

---

**Final Tip:** Keep your computer secure throughout its entire lifecycle. Proper disposal isn't just about the environment—it's about protecting your data for years to come!
